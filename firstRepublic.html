The Seduction of Mimi (1972)


This film has many elements involved in it from the mafia to the way communism had swept through and took a strong hold of Italy. Italy itself was under major cultural changes as well with the feminist and workers movements. This can be seen with various aspects of the film from the worker who died at Mimi's work and the various interactions with Fiorella. Of course this film had a very comical approach to what was happening.

Tango

This film shows a scene in which Mimi seduces Amalia with what seems to be the Tango. This dance style seems to have controversial questions as to where it originated and how it has routes in Italy. While the tango has a huge basis in Argentina, it seems to have captivated Italians for years. This could be due to the fact that many Italians had immigrated to Argentina in the late 1800s and early 1900s. Despite this film taking place in the 1970s the fact that Italy was still entranced byt it seems to show just how lasting of a dance type it is. In Serra's paper he states "The sentimentality that flows from tango melody derives from the feeling of nostalgia and longing for broken families and distant homes." This in itself is quite a beautiful statement and goes well with how this dance both captivates Mimi and Amalia and shows her longing for love.
